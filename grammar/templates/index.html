Parts of Speech

Understanding the different parts of speech is essential for identifying how words function in a sentence. Here are some common parts of speech with examples:

  • Noun (NN): A person, place, thing, or idea. Example: dog, city, happiness.
  • Verb (VB): An action or state of being. Example: run, is, seem.
  • Adjective (JJ): Describes or modifies a noun. Example: blue, quick, happy.
  • Adverb (RB): Modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverb. Example: quickly, very, well.
  • Pronoun (PRP): Replaces a noun. Example: he, they, it.
  • Preposition (IN): Shows the relationship between a noun (or pronoun) and another word in the sentence. Example: in, on, before.
  • Conjunction (CC): Connects words, phrases, or clauses. Example: and, but, or.
  • Determiner (DT): Introduces a noun. Example: the, a, this.

When taking the quiz, you will be asked to identify the correct part of speech for selected words in sentences.
